This game should feel like trying to stay human inside a day that wants you to be predictable.

## The Feeling Arc

1. Awakening: You start with routines that keep you functional, but the day already feels slightly wrong.
2. Journey: Public spaces and social noise create friction; small moments can feel heavier than they look.
3. Interview: Performance pressure peaks. You choose between safe scripts and honest responses.
4. Return: You carry the aftershock home and prepare to do it all again.

## What Players Usually Experience

- A constant push-pull between control and collapse.
- Relief when small self-directed choices are possible.
- Tension from knowing "success" is never fully achievable.
- Meaningful emotional contrast between compliance and authenticity.

## How To Read A Session

Treat each loop as an emotional pattern, not a win/lose test.

- Battery tells you how much capacity remains.
- Stability tells you how hard the system is pressing back.
- SIP tells you where personal agency appeared.

The important question after each loop is:
What did this day demand from the player, and what did the player refuse to give up?
